Welcom to the Twin Island:

San Pablo Island (Pong Gamay)

The San Pablo Island also known as "Pong Gamay", among the two island this much visitable in tourist because of the activities in the areas, such as kayaking and diving spot in the island. Here if you wanted to stay in a day you can rent a cottage with amount of 500 pesos and if you want over night you can rent cottage in amount of 850 pesos.

It seems that the island are gradually rising to the public and foreign tourist, the local goverment have an action or implementation to maintain the cleanliness of the island. This is strongly implemented by the local government unit to preserve and protect the island and the marine eco-system, and take note not just the two island but the whole shoreline of the province are prohibeted to throw any waste in the ocean.

San Pedro Island (Pong Daku)

The San Pedro Island also known as "Pong Daku" because this is much bigger than San Pablo Island, that is why it called "Pong Dako". This Island is perfect for the people who stressed in the city, meditation is perfect here because of their is no noise pollution and you only hear are the wind and the sound of the waves.

At the back of the island there is lagoon named Lelang Lagoon that were you can swim peacefully. On this part of San Pedro Island you can see the Gulf of Leyte the open ocean of the east part of leyte archipelago.

Calag-itan Fish Sanctuary

Calag-itan now is open for snorkeling to see the different kinds of marine animal protected by the LGU

The Calag-itan Fish Sanctuary is a home for the any marine animal that protected by the LGU (Local Government Unit) under the "AN ACT AMENDING CERTAIN SECTIONS OF REPUBLIC ACT NO. 8485, OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE ANIMAL WELFARE ACT OF 1998"

SECTION 1. Section 1 of Republic Act No. 8485 is hereby amended to read as follows;

SECTION 1. It is the purpose of this Act to protect and promote the welfare of all terrestrial, aquatic and marine animals in the Philippines by supervising and regulating the establishment and operations of all facilities utilized for breeding, maintaining, keeping, treating or training of all animals either as objects of trade or as household pets. For purposes of this Act, pet animal shall include birds.
